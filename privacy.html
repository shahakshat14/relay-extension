Privacy Policy

We built Relay to collect as little about you as possible — here's exactly what that means.

Last updated: April 19, 2026
The short version

Relay does not collect your name, email, or any personal information. It cannot read your bookmarks — they're encrypted on your device with your password before sync. We have no idea who you are, and that's intentional.

What we collect

When you use Relay, the following data is stored on our servers:

DataWhat it isCan we read it?
Encrypted vault Your bookmarks, encrypted on your device with AES-256-GCM before upload No — we don't have your password
Vault key hash A PBKDF2 hash derived from your username and a device-local salt. Not reversible to your username. No — it's a one-way hash
Browser UUID A random identifier generated on install, used only to enforce the 2-browser free plan limit Yes — but it's random, not tied to you
Sync log (hashed IP) A truncated SHA-256 hash of your IP for rate limiting. Deleted after 2 hours. No — not reversible to your IP

What we do not collect

We do not collect, store, or transmit:

Your nameNever collected
Your email addressNever collected
Your passwordNever leaves your device
Your usernameHashed before transmission — we can't reverse it
Your actual IP addressOnly a non-reversible hash, deleted in 2 hours
Your bookmark contentsEncrypted — we can't read them
Browsing historyNever collected
Usage analytics or telemetryNone — no analytics SDK, no tracking
Location dataNever collected

How encryption works

Your bookmarks are encrypted on your device before they ever leave. The process:

  1. Your password is used to derive an AES-256-GCM encryption key via PBKDF2 (600,000 iterations)
  2. Your bookmarks are encrypted with that key, with a random salt and IV generated fresh each sync
  3. Only the encrypted result is sent to our servers
What this means in practice: even if our entire database were compromised, an attacker would only obtain unreadable ciphertext. Without your password, the data is useless.

Third-party services

Supabase — our database provider, hosted in Canada (ca-central-1). Stores the encrypted vault and hashed identifiers. Cannot read your bookmark data. Subject to Supabase's privacy policy.

Stripe — payment processor for Relay Pro subscriptions. Handles all payment data directly. We never receive your card details. We link your subscription to your hashed vault key — not to a name or email. Subject to Stripe's privacy policy.

No other third-party services are used. There are no analytics SDKs, no error monitoring services, no advertising networks, and no tracking pixels anywhere in Relay.

Data retention

Your encrypted vault is retained until you delete your account. Sync history snapshots (Pro) are kept for 30 days then automatically deleted. Sync rate-limit logs are deleted after 2 hours.

When you delete your account through the extension, your vault, sync history, and browser registrations are permanently and immediately deleted from our servers.

Your rights

You can delete your account at any time from the Settings screen in the extension. This permanently removes all data we hold about you.

Because we cannot identify you (no email, no name), we cannot respond to subject access requests. The data we hold is either a random identifier or an encrypted blob we cannot read — there is nothing meaningful to provide.

Children

Relay is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children. If you believe a child has used Relay, please contact us.

Open source

Every privacy claim on this page can be verified by reading the source code at github.com/tridentcx/relay-extension. We have nothing to hide.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes materially, we will update the date at the top of this page. The extension will continue to work without any action from you.
